Aug 18 - 21

London, England

2022 World Championships

Sword & Shield - Pokemon GO

ExCeL London

Royal Victoria Dock, 1 Western Gateway

• The 2019 Pokemon World Championships returned to Washington D.C. for the second time in history. It was a three day event, where anyone who met the Championship Point threshold was invited to play in Day 1 on Friday. Of which consisted of 8 Rounds, anyone with a record of 5-0-3, or 6 wins, moved on to Day 2.

• Day 2 (Saturday) consisted of the above players that made it through a rough Day 1, as well as the Top X players from throughout the season, seperated by region, found below. Day 2 was also 8 Rounds long, but resulting in a Top 8 Cut. (118 Master division players competed in Day 2.) All Top 8 and Top 4 matches were played out that night, saving each division's Grand Finals for Sunday.

• This event also featured the "D.C. Open," a large, sanctioned, side event for players to get a head start to qualify for the next season's Worlds invite, which coincidently was paused and extended out into the 2022 season due to covid. Also at the venue was a pop-up Pokemon Center.
